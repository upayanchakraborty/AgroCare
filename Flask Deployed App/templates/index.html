Why is it necessary to detect disease in plants?

Plant diseases can harm crop growth, leading to lower yields and quality. Early detection is essential to prevent further damage and control the spread. By identifying diseases early, farmers can take quick action, reducing the need for costly treatments and excessive pesticide use. This helps conserve resources and promotes healthier plants. Early diagnosis also allows farmers to choose the best solutions for better crop quality and higher yields. Ultimately, early detection supports sustainable farming and food security, ensuring a stable and healthy food supply for future generations.
